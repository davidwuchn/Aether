  <action>
Add a `<pheromone_protocol>` section to each of the three agent definition files. The section must be placed AFTER the `<critical_rules>` section and BEFORE the `<return_format>` section in each file.

**Base protocol (shared by all three agents):**
The protocol explains that the worker's spawn context may include a `--- COMPACT SIGNALS ---` or `--- ACTIVE SIGNALS ---` section containing colony guidance injected by the Queen via colony-prime. It covers three signal types:

- REDIRECT (HARD CONSTRAINTS - MUST follow): Non-negotiable avoidance instructions. If a REDIRECT says "avoid pattern X", the worker MUST NOT use pattern X. REDIRECTs marked `[error-pattern]` come from repeated colony failures (midden threshold) -- treat as lessons learned. Acknowledge each REDIRECT in the output summary.
- FOCUS (Pay attention to): Attention directives. Prioritize the indicated area. When choosing between approaches, prefer the one aligned with active FOCUS signals.
- FEEDBACK (Flexible guidance): Calibration signals from past experience. Consider when making judgment calls. May deviate with good reason but note the deviation.

Include an acknowledgment instruction: if any signals were present in spawn context, include a brief note in the summary field of the return JSON indicating which signals were observed and how they influenced the work.

**Agent-specific adaptations (add after the shared base):**

For **builder**: REDIRECT signals constrain implementation choices (do not use the flagged pattern). FOCUS signals influence which areas get extra test coverage. FEEDBACK signals adjust coding patterns.

For **watcher**: REDIRECT signals become verification checkpoints -- verify the avoided pattern was indeed avoided by the implementation. FOCUS signals direct which areas receive deeper scrutiny in the quality assessment. FEEDBACK signals influence quality scoring weights.

For **scout**: REDIRECT signals constrain research scope -- do not recommend patterns that are currently redirected. FOCUS signals prioritize research areas. FEEDBACK signals weight source credibility.

**Keep the total section under 40 lines per agent.** Workers are LLMs; they understand intent-based instructions. Do not over-prescribe conditional logic.

**Do NOT:**
- Instruct workers to parse JSON from pheromones.json directly (the prompt_section is pre-formatted markdown)
- Instruct workers to call pheromone-read or any bash commands to read signals (signals are injected into their prompt)
- Change any existing behavior (TDD Iron Law, Evidence Iron Law, No Write tools for Watcher/Scout, etc.)
- Exceed 40 lines for the pheromone_protocol section
